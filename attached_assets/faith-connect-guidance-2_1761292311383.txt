## Session 1: ANCHORED IN GOD'S PURPOSE

### Biblical Foundation
**Jeremiah 1:4-5** - "Now the word of the Lord came to me saying, 'Before I formed you in the womb I knew you, and before you were born I consecrated you; I have appointed you a prophet to the nations.'"

**Ephesians 2:10** - "For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand so that we would walk in them."

### Theological Context
Jeremiah's calling demonstrates that God's purpose precedes our existence. The Hebrew word for "knew" (yada) implies intimate, relational knowledge. For men today, this means our identity isn't found in career achievements, physical strength, or social status, but in being known and called by God before time began.

### Practical Application for Men
1. **Identity Audit**: List 5 ways you currently define yourself. Compare them with how Scripture defines you.
2. **Purpose Alignment**: Evaluate your weekly schedule - what percentage reflects eternal purposes versus temporary achievements?
3. **Legacy Building**: Identify one spiritual legacy you want to leave for your family.

### Personal Reflection Questions
- What masculine stereotypes pressure you to find identity in performance rather than Christ?
- How does knowing God formed you with intention change how you approach daily challenges?
- Where have you been "building your identity on shaky ground" as Rev. Chan described?

## Session 2: ASSURED BY GOD'S PRESENCE

### Biblical Foundation
**Jeremiah 1:7-8** - "But the Lord said to me, 'Do not say, "I am a youth," because everywhere I send you, you shall go, and all that I command you, you shall speak. Do not be afraid of them, for I am with you to deliver you,' declares the Lord."

**Isaiah 41:10** - "Do not fear, for I am with you; do not be dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you, I will help you, I will uphold you with My righteous right hand."

### Theological Context
Jeremiah's fear of inadequacy (being "too young") mirrors modern men's fears of not measuring up. God's response isn't to remove the challenge but to provide His presence. The Hebrew word for "with you" (immak) implies active, engaged presence - God doesn't watch from sidelines but enters the battle with us.

### Practical Application for Men
1. **Fear Inventory**: Identify 3 areas where fear hinders your obedience to God's calling.
2. **Presence Reminders**: Create daily triggers (phone alarms, sticky notes) reminding you "God is with me."
3. **Brotherhood Accountability**: Share one fear with another Christian man this week and pray together.

### Personal Reflection Questions
- What excuses do you make for avoiding God's calling, similar to Jeremiah's "I am too young"?
- How does the cultural pressure to "be strong" prevent you from relying on God's strength?
- When have you experienced God's presence most tangibly in difficult circumstances?

## Session 3: ANTICIPATING GOD'S PROMISES

### Biblical Foundation
**Jeremiah 1:10** - "See, I have appointed you this day over the nations and over the kingdoms, to pluck up and to break down, to destroy and to overthrow, to build and to plant."

**Lamentations 3:21-23** - "This I recall to my mind, therefore I have hope. The Lord's lovingkindnesses indeed never cease, for His compassions never fail. They are new every morning; great is Your faithfulness."

### Theological Context
Jeremiah's ministry involved both judgment ("pluck up, break down") and restoration ("build, plant"). Similarly, men today must balance confronting sin while building godly lives. The anticipation isn't passive waiting but active hope based on God's character.

### Practical Application for Men
1. **Promise Journal**: Start recording God's promises and when you see them fulfilled.
2. **Eternal Perspective**: When facing disappointment, ask "How will this look in 10 years? In eternity?"
3. **Hope Declaration**: Memorize and declare [Jeremiah 29:11](bible://Jeremiah 29:11) when discouraged.

### Personal Reflection Questions
- What God-given dream have you stopped anticipating because of delays or opposition?
- How does our culture's instant gratification mindset conflict with biblical perseverance?
- Which of Jeremiah's sufferings (rejection, imprisonment, exile) do you most relate to, and why?
