FISHERS OF MEN

TITLE & THEME OVERVIEW
Title: Built to Last: Finding Strength When You Want to Quit
Subtitle: How God's Purpose, Presence, and Promises Empower Men to Persevere

Central Message: Every man faces moments when he wants to quit - whether in his career, marriage, parenting, or personal faith. This study examines Jeremiah's calling to discover how God equips men to persevere through workplace stress, financial pressure, family conflicts, and personal failures. Just as God formed Jeremiah for his purpose before birth, He has designed you with specific strength for your unique battles.

Key Verse: Jeremiah 1:19 (NASB): "They will fight against you, but they will not overcome you, for I am with you to deliver you," declares the Lord.

Direct Connection to Men's Issues: This passage speaks directly to men feeling overwhelmed by the pressure to provide, workplace competition that feels unfair, the isolation of leadership responsibilities, the fear of failing as a husband or father, and the temptation to compromise integrity when under stress. God's promise to Jeremiah meets men right where they struggle most.

THREE CORE TEACHING SECTIONS

SECTION ONE: ANCHORED IN GOD'S PURPOSE WHEN YOU FEEL LIKE A FAILURE
Scripture: Jeremiah 1:4-5 (NASB): "Now the word of the Lord came to me saying, 'Before I formed you in the womb I knew you, and before you were born I consecrated you; I have appointed you a prophet to the nations.'"

Teaching Point: When you're passed over for promotion at work or feel inadequate as a provider, this passage reminds us that our worth isn't defined by our performance but by God's pre-ordained purpose. Just as Jeremiah was set apart before birth, your identity as a man isn't based on your salary, job title, or even your success as a husband and father. Many men build their identity on shaky ground - career achievements, sexual conquests, or financial status - but God says you were designed for eternal significance long before you faced your first performance review or financial setback.

Discussion Questions:
1. What recent situation made you question your competence as a man - whether at work, in your marriage, or as a father?
2. How does the pressure to "measure up" to other men affect your daily decisions and emotional health?
3. When have you felt like Jeremiah - too young, inexperienced, or unqualified for what God was calling you to do?
4. What practical difference would it make in your Monday morning mindset if you truly believed God designed you specifically for your current responsibilities?

Practical Application:
This week, when you feel inadequate at work or home, stop and declare: "God formed me for this moment." Write Jeremiah 1:5 on a notecard for your desk or dashboard. Initiate one honest conversation with your wife or a trusted friend about an area where you feel like you're failing, and ask for prayer support.

SECTION TWO: ASSURED BY GOD'S PRESENCE WHEN YOU'RE FIGHTING ALONE
Scripture: Jeremiah 1:7-8 (NASB): "But the Lord said to me, 'Do not say, 'I am a youth,' because everywhere I send you, you shall go, and all that I command you, you shall speak. Do not be afraid of them, for I am with you to deliver you,' declares the Lord."

Teaching Point: Men often bear burdens alone - the stress of providing, the weight of leadership decisions, the pressure to have all the answers. We make excuses like Jeremiah did, saying we're not ready, not equipped, or too overwhelmed. But God's response to every excuse is His presence. When you're facing impossible deadlines at work, dealing with rebellious children, or struggling with secret sins, God doesn't say "try harder" - He says "I am with you." The same God who empowered Jeremiah to face kings and armies stands with you in your boardroom, your living room, and your private battles.

Discussion Questions:
1. Where do you feel most alone in your responsibilities as a man right now?
2. What excuses have you been making to avoid difficult conversations or responsibilities God might be calling you to address?
3. How does the isolation of leadership - at work or home - affect your decision-making and emotional health?
4. When has God proven His presence in a situation where you felt completely overwhelmed and alone?

Practical Application:
Identify one area where you've been making excuses and take one step of obedience this week. Text another man from this group when you feel overwhelmed rather than withdrawing. Practice saying "God is with me in this" before entering stressful situations at work or home.

SECTION THREE: ANTICIPATING GOD'S PROMISES WHEN YOU CAN'T SEE THE WAY FORWARD
Scripture: Jeremiah 1:10 (NASB): "See, I have appointed you this day over the nations and over the kingdoms, to pluck up and to break down, to destroy and to overthrow, to build and to plant."

Teaching Point: Jeremiah faced years of opposition with little visible success, much like men who work hard but see little fruit - the businessman whose company struggles despite his efforts, the father who disciplines but sees no change in his children, the husband who serves his wife but feels unappreciated. God's promise to Jeremiah included both tearing down and building up, both difficulty and restoration. Your perseverance today plants seeds for future harvest, even when you can't see immediate results.
